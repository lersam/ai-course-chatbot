**Question:** How much did Milverton once pay a footman for a two-line note that resulted in the ruin of a noble family?

**Question:** Why is Lady Eva Brackwell being blackmailed by Milverton?
**Answer:** Milverton possesses several "imprudent" letters she wrote to a young squire, which he threatens to send to her future husband, the Earl of Dovercourt, to break off their impending marriage.

**Question:** What alias does Sherlock Holmes use to gain information about Milverton’s household, and what is his supposed occupation?
**Answer:** He uses the name "Escott" and pretends to be a plumber with a rising business.

**Question:** What specific item does Watson offer to make to help them hide their identities during the burglary?
**Answer:** Watson offers to make a couple of masks out of black silk.

**Question:** While hiding behind the curtains in the study, what specific detail does Watson notice about the safe that worries him?
**Answer:** Watson notices that the door of the safe is "imperfectly closed" and fears Milverton might observe it at any moment.

**Question:** According to the woman who kills Milverton, what happened to her husband after Milverton sent him her letters?
**Answer:** She states that her husband, whom she described as the noblest gentleman, "broke his gallant heart and died."

**Question:** After the woman flees the scene, what does Holmes do with the contents of Milverton's safe?
**Answer:** Holmes empties the safe and burns all the bundles of letters in the fire to ensure they can no longer be used for blackmail.

**Question:** What reason does Holmes give Inspector Lestrade for refusing to assist in the investigation of Milverton’s murder?
**Answer:** Holmes states that his sympathies are with the criminals rather than the victim, believing that some crimes justify private revenge when the law cannot touch them.

**Question:** Where do Holmes and Watson eventually see a photograph that confirms the identity of the woman who killed Milverton?
**Answer:** They see her photograph in a shop window near Regent Circus that is filled with pictures of celebrities and beauties.
